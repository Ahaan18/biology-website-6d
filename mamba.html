About black mamba

A black mamba (Dendroaspis polylepis) is a species of the mamba snakes group. It is known for its size, quickness and extremely strong venom. It is usually found in Africa and is considered the most dangerous snake of the continent. The average black mamba is about 2-2.5 meters long and can go up to 4.3 meters. The color of this snake isnt black, instead it ranges from grey to dark brown. This snake is multicelluar, eukaryotic, lacking a cell wall and are heterotrophic meaning it can't produce its own food and need to rely on external sources to get food. The black mamba is generally shy and nervous and will use its incredible speed to escape threats. It is not that important because it is responsible for lots of human deaths and is considered the deadliest snake of Africa.

Facts:

1. If the mamba is concerned, it will rear up and threaten with its open mouth

2. Its venom is so strong that it is possible that one or two drops of it can easily kill most humans. Even though most bites are fatal, it is responsible for only a small number of deaths.

Its habitat

The black mamba lives in south and east African savannas, rocky hills and open woodlands. They are found in both tropical and temperate regions. Their habitat is critically endangered and is protected 24%. It is threatened because of human actions like poaching, logging, conversion to agriculture and climate change

Movement:

The black mamba slithers quickly in short bursts over level ground and can zoom about one-third of their bodies off the ground.

Respiration:

Black mambas have an extendible trachea or windpipe which allows them to breathe while ingesting prey

Sensitivity:

Black mambas have excellent vision which helps them track prey when they are hunting. They are patient hunters and will wait until they will get the opportunity to make a strike

Growth:

Black mambas grow upto 14 feet long but their average length is around 8 feet. They can live upto 11 years in the wild!

Reproduction:

Black mambas have sexual reproduction. Male black mambas will fight for the affection of female ones. Then after this is done, females will lay around 6 to 25 eggs in a damp warm burrow.

Excretion:

The black mamba excretes venom using its fixed and hollow fangs and they use hypodermic needles to inject venom into its prey.

Nutrition:

The black mamba is a heterotrophe. It is a carnivore which means it will hunt for its food and eat other animals.
